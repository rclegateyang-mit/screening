here are two worker-specific files in the LEHD:

1. EHF (Employment History Files)
The EHF is essentially the "master list" of employment for an individual. It is person-centric.

Unit of Observation: One record per person, per year.
Purpose: It tracks an individual's longitudinal path across the labor market.
Data Content: It aggregates all earnings for a person within a given year. It’s designed to show a person's total labor market engagement, including transitions between jobs, periods of unemployment, and total annual earnings.
Best Use Case: Use this if your research is focused on worker outcomes, such as "How do a worker's total earnings change over 10 years?" or "Does a worker stay in the same industry over time?"


2. JHF (Job History Files)
The JHF is job-centric. It focuses on the specific link between a worker and a specific employer (SEIN - State Employer Identification Number).

Unit of Observation: One record per unique job (worker-firm pair).
Purpose: It documents the characteristics of the "job" itself. A worker who has three different employers in one year will have one EHF record for that year but multiple JHF records.
Data Content: It includes start dates, end dates, and quarterly earnings specifically tied to that employer. It often contains "beginning-of-quarter" and "end-of-quarter" employment indicators to help identify job stability.
Best Use Case: Use this if your research is focused on firm-worker dynamics, such as "What is the average tenure of employees at this firm?" or "How many people were hired and then left within the same quarter?"
